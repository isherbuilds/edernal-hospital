# HMS (working name)

An AI-native Hospital Management System for Indian mid-size hospitals. The first release is a workflow-first OPD pilot at one design-partner hospital; AI scribe, ABDM, and medication-safety features layer on after the operational loop is proven.

## Language

### People and places

**Tenant**:
One customer hospital organization. The unit of data isolation, configuration, and billing.
_Avoid_: org, hospital account, customer (in code)

**Facility**:
A physical site belonging to a Tenant. The pilot Tenant has one Facility.
_Avoid_: branch, location, site

**Patient**:
A person receiving care, registered by the front desk. Identified internally by a system ID; ABHA is an optional external identifier slot, unused at pilot.

**Practitioner**:
A licensed clinician (doctor) who conducts Encounters and signs Consult Notes and Prescriptions.
_Avoid_: provider, physician (in code)

**Staff User**:
Any authenticated hospital user: front desk, practitioner, billing, pharmacy/lab operator, or hospital admin. One human, one account — no shared logins.

**Design Partner**:
A hospital with a signed pilot agreement that uses the system in real operations and gives structured feedback.

### The OPD loop

**Encounter**:
One OPD visit by a Patient: created at check-in, closed after billing. The anchor for notes, prescriptions, orders, and charges.
_Avoid_: visit, consultation (as a data term), appointment (different concept)

**Token**:
The queue number issued to a Patient for a specific Practitioner on a given day. Tokens order the Queue.

**Queue**:
The live, per-practitioner list of waiting Tokens with statuses (waiting, in-consult, done, skipped).
_Avoid_: waitlist, line

**Consult Note**:
The Practitioner's clinical record for an Encounter (complaints, findings, diagnosis, advice). Written via template or free text at pilot; AI-drafted later.
_Avoid_: SOAP note (aspirational structure, not a data term yet), composition

**Prescription**:
The list of medications the Practitioner prescribes in an Encounter, composed from the Formulary or free text, signed, then printed. Signed Prescriptions are immutable; corrections create a superseding Prescription.
_Avoid_: Rx (in code), e-Rx (implies digital transmission — pilot only prints)

**Formulary**:
The hospital's own list of medications available for quick-pick prescribing. Not a national drug dictionary; no interaction rules at pilot.
_Avoid_: drug dictionary, drug catalog

**Order**:
A Practitioner's request for a pharmacy item or lab test in an Encounter. Orders route to a Work Queue and generate a Charge. The system tracks order status only — inventory lives in the hospital's existing software.
_Avoid_: service request, requisition

**Work Queue**:
The pharmacy or lab screen listing pending Orders to be marked in-progress / completed.

**Sign-off**:
The explicit act by which a Practitioner makes a Consult Note or Prescription final. Anything unsigned is Preliminary and cannot be printed or shared.

**Preliminary**:
The state of any clinical artifact before Sign-off. Preliminary artifacts are visible only inside the care workflow.
_Avoid_: draft (UI label is fine; the state name in code is preliminary)

### Money

**Service Catalog**:
The Tenant's list of billable items (consultations, procedures, lab tests, pharmacy items) with prices and GST rates.
_Avoid_: tariff (legacy import term only)

**Charge**:
One billable line generated by an Encounter event (consult fee, Order). Charges accumulate on the Draft Bill.
_Avoid_: bill line, charge capture

**Draft Bill**:
The running, editable total of Charges for an Encounter before finalization.

**Invoice**:
The finalized, numbered, GST-compliant bill. Immutable once issued; corrections use credit notes.
_Avoid_: bill (ambiguous with Draft Bill)

**Payment**:
Money received against an Invoice: cash or manual UPI reference at pilot. Gateway integration is post-pilot.

**Billed Today**:
The total finalized Invoice value for the business day, net of discounts and credit notes. This answers what the hospital charged today, not what it collected.
_Avoid_: earned today

**Collected Today**:
The total Payments received during the business day, split by mode (cash, manual UPI/bank, credit). This answers cash movement, not billable work performed.
_Avoid_: earned today

**Discounts Given Today**:
The total discount value approved or applied during the business day, shown separately from Billed Today so discounts are visible to the owner.

**Ordered-not-billed**:
The revenue-leakage report listing completed Orders whose Charges never reached an Invoice.

**Accountant Export**:
The CSV registers or connector batches (invoices, collections, day-close summary, GST fields, and later inventory valuation) HMS produces for the hospital's accountant/CA. The seam can target Tally, Edernal Books, CSV, or another configured accounting destination. HMS never contains a general ledger.
_Avoid_: accounting module, GL (as an HMS feature)

**Day-close Posting**:
The future one-way, aggregate, PHI-free accounting feed from HMS day-close into the configured accounting connector. It keeps separate `occurredAt`, `businessDate`, `exportedAt`, and `postedAt` timestamps so 24/7 hospital operations do not depend on a midnight cutoff.

### Trust

**PHI**:
Protected Health Information — any data identifying a Patient or describing their health or payments. Determines audit, logging, and encryption obligations.

**Audit Event**:
The append-only record of who did what to which PHI, when, in which Tenant. Every PHI read and write emits one.
_Avoid_: log (operational logs are a separate, PHI-free stream)

**Trust Envelope**:
The set of security controls that must pass before real Patient data enters the system: auth and roles, tested tenant isolation, Audit Events, encryption at rest, PHI-safe logs, tested backups.

**Risk Register**:
The written list of deliberately deferred controls (break-glass, purpose-of-use engine, per-tenant KMS, hash-chained audit) with founder risk acceptance.

**PHI-safe logging**:
The rule that operational logs, traces, and error messages never contain Patient identifiers or clinical text — only IDs, codes, and metrics.

### Deferred concepts (defined so we stop redefining them)

**ABHA**:
A patient's national health ID under ABDM. At pilot: an empty identifier slot on Patient.

**ABDM**:
India's national health-data exchange network. Post-pilot integration; requires FHIR bundles and consent artefacts.

**FHIR**:
The interoperability standard for exchanging health data. In this system FHIR is an export/translation seam for ABDM — not the internal storage format.

**Scribe**:
The future ambient AI documentation feature (consented audio → transcript → draft Consult Note). Post-pilot.

**Break-glass**:
Future emergency access mechanism allowing a clinician to open an unassigned Patient chart with a recorded reason and elevated audit.
